Products

At Hiraeth Guitars we use only the finest timbers from around the globe. All wood is hand selected by our shop foreman for both a tight grain and consitent colour. We season all timbers for a minimum of two-years in our humdity and climate controlled timber warehouse. This ensures that only the optimum materials are used in any of our builds, regardless of the ange or cost. We also believe strongly in ethical and sustainable harvesting of tonewoods; choosing to source our materials only from certified saw-mills. We have a wide variety of wood options available for your build, allowing for a diverse range of tonal possibilities and aesthetic options. Below are some of the material and body options for our standard range. For anything more exotic, please get in touch; we'd be happy to discuss your aims.

Wood Options - Back and Sides

Indian Rosewood

Indian rosewood’s wide frequency range at both ends of the tonal spectrum has made it one of the most sought-after and musically rich tonewoods. Its deep bass response can assert a throaty growl, while bright, sparkling treble notes ring out with bell-like clarity. A true tonewood classic!

Mahogany

Mahogany is renowned for its substantial midrange character, creating a strong fundamental tone often described as “punchy,” or “woody,” because it doesn’t produce a lot of ringing overtones. Mahogany’s warm, earthy voice has been featured on a plethora of roots music recordings over the years.

Maple

Maple has a long association as a very bright acoustic tonewood, but Hiraeth's maple built guitars are voiced to bring out greater warmth, richness and sustain. This broadens maple’s tonal range significantly from a one-dimensional sound to a more musical and versatile voice.

Wood Options - Top

Sitka Spruce

The most widely used top wood on the planet, and for very good reason; Sitka spruce is the perfect all-rounder. Spruce produces a clear and full tone, with sparkling trebles and deep bass. Low cost and versatile performance, Sitka spruce is at home in any genre or style.

Adirondack Spruce

Adirondack spruce, otherwise known as "red spruce" was once the most popular tonewood on the planet, until overharvesting caused it to run scarce. Its stiffness and light weight give it an immense projection and headroom, and produces a bright timbre rich in overtones and harmonics.

Cedar

Famed for it's use on classical guitars, Cedar is equally as adapt on steel-string models. A relatively soft wood; Cedar produces a warm, intimate tone, rich in overtones and highly balanced. This makes it especially useful for fingerstyle guitar. Its warm colour also result in a pleasing aesthetic.

Body Shapes

Jumbo

The largest instrument we make here at Hiraeth Guitars; the Jumbo body style has a large bout resulting in a massive sound with plenty of low-end. The fantastic projection of this body shape is enough to fill a room with the lightest strum. A real canon!

OM

The OM or orchestra model has been a firm favourite in the world of guitars ever since it's inception in 1929. It's medium size gives it a well balanced sound, making it the perfect fingerstyle instrument, although it is equally adapt as a rhythm guitar.

Dreadnaught

Renowned for it's rich tone and loud volume, the dreadnaught remains one of the most popular acoustic guitar shapes ever. It's large size and projection make this the perfect rhythm instrument; capable of keeping up with even the loudest instruments.
